### G1. pull-status-over-silent-channels — RANK 1

- **Thesis:** run/task status is PULLED over long-lived, mostly-silent SSH
  channels (client polls, remote walks), so every middlebox, reaper,
  quota, and latency property of a silent channel becomes a run-state
  failure mode.
- **Fired symptoms retired:** r12-f24 (NAT idle-drop severs every
  long-silent leg at ~100s; the 40-minute reporter walk; misattributed
  twice), r12-f20 (login-node fork exhaustion from orphaned remote halves
  of severed channels; self-service-unrecoverable at worst), r12-f19 (S3
  watch read ssh timeouts as run death, fired a terminal harvest at a live
  array), r12-f16 (detached S2 staging/liveness blindness — zero liveness
  during a 14-min wedge), r12-f3 (no liveness surface on the MCP link;
  2×~10-min wedges). Contributory/overlap: #8 (the idle reaper was the
  second severing mechanism — primary G4), #3 (the 25–40-min silent
  reporter walk the channel must survive — primary G13).
- **Diagnosis cost (cited):** the 2026-07-11 saga — "one generator, a full
  day" (audit doc); r12-f24: "misattributed twice before the evidence
  converged" (first to fork exhaustion, then to the stale wheel); measured
  control run 40m26s wall for <2 CPU-minutes; r12-f3: ~10 min of human
  attention ×2 with zero mechanical disclosure.
- **Upstream alternative:** crash-only monitoring / cluster-announces — the
  cluster ANNOUNCES state transitions (task markers in ONE directory, one
  readdir) instead of the client pulling status over a channel that must
  stay alive; the question stops being asked over a channel at all.
  Phase 1 shipped the same day the cluster diagnosed it. Belt fixes
  already landed under it: keepalives in ssh_argv, remote-deadline wrapper
  (orphans self-destruct), #8 inflight fix, frozen-manifest reporter,
  stray-sweep verb.
- **Migration cost:** medium — announce kernel (cluster-side markers +
  reader) and watch consumption remain; the belt layer is landed. Phase 1
  shipped 2026-07-11.
- **Flip trigger (scope-by-constraint):** pull-over-ssh was the ship-time
  call (no cluster-side agent, works on day one against any cluster). The
  flip is recorded and partially fired: announce kernel replacing the pull
  reporter for the heavy walk (r12-f24: "the announce kernel … is the fix
  that carries the weight"); steady state when the watch consumes
  announcements end-to-end (post-run-13 window).
- **Confidence:** high (the user-recorded exemplar; membership verified).

  hand-edited in the journal — surgery with no locking/provenance),
  r12-f25 (run-12 close-out required journal surgery bypassing the

- **Upstream alternative:** the determinism boundary applied to its last

  Every transition the system can make on probed evidence must also be

- **Migration cost:** medium-high — new verbs + chain-table arms; design
  done (pr5 §3–§4); backstops (waves 2–4, 7, 5.3) already shipped so the
  surface change has a net under it.
- **Flip trigger:** the backstop net is landed (pr5 §5 table); the flip is
  run-13 build bandwidth for 5.1/5.2 + the two run-12 verbs (ruling 6).
- **Confidence:** high (two proving runs converge on it independently;

### G3. unbound-evidence-matching (forensic text vs bound/scoped capture) — RANK 3

- **Thesis:** attestations, approvals, and claims are verified by
  substring/regex/name-shape/latest-record matching over UNBOUND streams
  (serialized JSON, the whole utterance log, transcripts, filenames, the
  shared journal) instead of evidence captured — or at least scoped —
  BOUND to the exact target it answers. Deriving intent from an
  unstructured stream is inherently forensic (r12 design note); every
  forensic rule is a fresh false-pass/false-block surface.
- **Fired symptoms retired:** r12-f10 (standing prompts satisfied sign-off
  gates: two FALSE human attestations journaled, remediation re-sign
  required), r12-f9 (T8 gate had no utterance-log tier — E-render-primary
  structurally dead), r12-f21 + #1 + #23 (one incident + two sweep sites:
  a consumed greenlight is indistinguishable from a fresh one — latest-y
  scans with no boundary/temporal binding; per-turn forced ticks became
  an SSH amplifier during the fork-exhaustion crisis), #12 ('verified'
  JSON KEY substring makes the misrelay guard dead once any S2 brief
  exists), #22 (clicked labels reconstructed by comma-split — model text
  laundered into the trust anchor), #39 (regex drops the minus sign;
  truthful relays blocked), #54/#55 (transcript trailing-run and run-id
  prefix-substring false positives), #56 (hook fires on a command-string
  substring, ignoring the structured tool_response; injects stale
  envelopes), #53/#70 (run ownership parsed from filename shape while the
  record's own run_id field is ignored — foreign terminals sealed into
  another run's story/dossier), #60 ('dispatch.py' bare-substring
  executor refusal). Supporting audit findings: the B4 sweep's
  scope-unlock word-overlap and naming-only revoke gates (whole-log, no
  anchor).
- **Diagnosis cost (cited):** r12-f10's false attestations + user
  re-signing; r12-f21 "blocked EVERY turn end … another SSH volley at the
  exhausted login node"; pr5-f15's ~6 correction round-trips are the same
  forensic-relay tax; the B4 sweep itself (a full gate-by-gate audit) is
  the standing maintenance cost of the forensic tier.
- **Upstream alternative:** bound capture (`docs/design/bound-capture.md`,
  banked): the surface that elicits knows exactly what it elicits for and
  journals the utterance BOUND to that (audit_id, section, view_sha) —
  zero token-matching. Where binding surfaces don't exist yet: (a) the
  shared ts>=anchor filter (finding-10 pattern generalized; B4 fix-wave
  is dispatch-ready), (b) boundary scoping for greenlight consumption
  (resolved.next_block must name the parked boundary; _park stamps the
  consumed decision's identity), (c) value-semantic evidence sets instead
  of serialized-JSON substrings, (d) structured payloads over joined
  strings (#22), (e) record-field identity over name-shape parses
  (#53/#70), (f) word-boundary fencing where text matching is genuinely
  unavoidable. The sha-prefix leg (B4: temporal binding by vocabulary
  impossibility) is the third design lever.
- **Migration cost:** low for anchors/scoping (one shared helper applied
  at ~6 gates + the marker stamp); medium for bound capture (elicitation-
  response record schema; the popup channel is proven live — r12-f11).
- **Flip trigger:** forensic machinery existed because attestation was
  retrofitted onto a general chat log — the only out-of-band channel
  pre-elicitation. The elicitation popup FIRING live (r12-f11) is the
  constraint flip; bound capture becomes primary at popup-capable seats,
  the forensic tier demotes to the honestly-weaker fallback it is.
- **Confidence:** high (the dockets name the generator themselves; 12+
  independent symptoms).

### G4. hand-rolled-connection-lifecycle (vs library-native) — RANK 4

- **Thesis:** the framework re-implements transport-lifecycle mechanisms
  its libraries already own (idle management, lease/TTL bookkeeping,
  keepalive policy); each hand-rolled mechanism mints failure modes no
  library-native stack has.
- **Fired symptoms retired:** #8 (idle reaper measures idleness from last
  COMPLETION → severs any >120s in-flight command, then silently
  re-executes it one-shot — duplicate execution of non-idempotent remote
  commands; this was the SECOND severing mechanism of the 2026-07-11
  night), #35 (ssh_slots: fixed TTL below the worst legitimate hold +
  ownership-blind release → cascading over-admission of the host the
  limiter protects), r12-f16 (stale detach `lease.lock` with a blocking,
  timeout-less acquire freezes every successor worker), r12-f24
  library-boundary lesson (asyncssh's native keepalives/lifecycle were
  CORRECT; the one hand-rolled piece layered on top is where the sever
  lived).
- **Diagnosis cost (cited):** r12-f24 addendum — a second severing
  mechanism had to be hunted the same night, with keepalives already
  active ("misattributed twice"); r12-f16: 14+ min frozen worker + the
  forensics to prove 0.015s CPU; #8's duplicate-side-effect hazard
  (combiner writes, slow qsub) is unbounded.
- **Upstream alternative (ruled, B16):** shrink hand-rolled lifecycle to
  what NO library can know — the ban-risk breaker and connection-RATE
  courtesy are cluster-social POLICY and stay; the MECHANISMS (idle
  management, keepalives, multiplexing, lease/TTL/pid bookkeeping) are
  the library's (asyncssh; the filelock/psutil outsourcing precedent for
  leases and slots). ssh_slots is the named next suspect.
- **Migration cost:** medium — delete/outsource the reaper class of code,
  re-home slot leases on a library lease; the #8 inflight-counter belt is
  already landed.
- **Flip trigger (recorded in r12-f24):** the native-binary-first verdict
  was a SHIP-TIME call (Windows named-pipe agent, engine maturity); the
  steady-state verdict is engine-first with library-native lifecycle —
  "the trigger that flips it is the #8 inflight fix + this lesson landing
  in a released wheel." Ruled post-run-13 (ruling 5 confirms schedule).
- **Confidence:** high.

### G5. win32-as-retrofit (a second, hand-maintained transport stack) — RANK 5

- **Thesis:** the Windows control plane rides separately-implemented
  fallback paths (tar|ssh, MSYS-rsync operand quirks, locale defaults,
  home-dir resolution) retrofitted onto a POSIX-first design; every
  POSIX-side fix must be manually mirrored, and unmirrored sites are
  total breakage on the platform.
- **Fired symptoms retired:** r12-f17 (the 8.7GB stage broke THREE ways:
  argv-limit WinError 206 on the tar member list, MSYS drive-colon on
  rsync src, workers exiting without a terminal — legs 1+2 fixed live),
  r12-f13 (mcp-serve decoded stdin as cp1252 — human text mojibaked
  inside the server), #10/#11 (the drive-colon translation exists ONLY at
  rsync_push; _rsync_deploy and rsync_pull never got it — every deploy
  and every pull fails with MSYS rsync installed), #9 (rsync-less push:
  unbounded pump join deadlocks forever when ssh exits early), #64
  (RSYNC_RSH space-joins a spaced binary path — Program Files breaks
  every rsync), #65 (Path.home() RuntimeError crashes every ssh_argv in
  stripped-env service contexts). Overlap: #26 (PowerShell UTF-16 spec —
  primary G7).
- **Diagnosis cost (cited):** r12-f17 — "an hour of live workaround
  archaeology" mid-run, six dead-worker alerts + an hour of forensics on
  leg 3, plus improvised system mutations (copied MSYS DLLs) the conduct
  ledger had to clean up.
- **Upstream alternative:** ONE local-operand translation seam
  (`_msys_local` / `_rsync_local_arg`) that every rsync/tar/ssh local
  operand routes through; a win32-parametrized contract-test matrix over
  every transport entry point (push, pull, deploy, RSH env); locale/
  encoding pinned at the process boundary (stdin joined the UTF-8
  reconfigure). Windows stops being per-site patches and becomes a
  gating test axis.
- **Migration cost:** low-medium — the helpers are cheap (fix sketches
  already factor them); the real cost is the win32 CI/test leg.
- **Flip trigger:** POSIX-first was the ship-time constraint (real
  clusters are POSIX; the dev loop wasn't on Windows CI). Flips when the
  win32 parametrized matrix gates merges — mirroring stops being memory.
- **Confidence:** high.

### G14. in-band-absence-collapse (None/empty conflated with 'not declared') — RANK 14

- **Thesis:** optional fields use in-band falsy sentinels, so
  "legitimately None/empty" collapses into "absent" at exactly the
  boundaries where the distinction is the security/correctness content.
- **Fired symptoms retired:** #37 (unattributed None actor withdraws an
  unattributed challenge — None != None is False; the anonymous-

  delegation list `{"registration": []}` treated as unrestricted —
  fail-open on an authorization check), #30 (a legitimately-pickled None
  checkpoint state reads as 'no checkpoint' — silent restart from 0
  discarding the resume point the canary certified restorable).
- **Diagnosis cost:** sweep-caught; two are trust-boundary fail-opens.
- **Upstream alternative:** absent-vs-declared distinguished
  structurally: `policy.get(block) is None` vs `== []`; explicit
  refusal of None actors at trust boundaries (mirroring the verdict
  leg); out-of-band discriminators for state (next_iteration, not
  `state is None`). Ruling 4 covers the one genuinely open arm.
- **Migration cost:** low.
- **Flip trigger:** immediate except the ruling-gated arm.
- **Confidence:** medium.

### G15. success-only-cap-accounting — RANK 15

- **Thesis:** bounded-attempts backstops count attempts from success-only
  records, bumped after the act — so failure modes under-count and the
  fail-loud cap LOOSENS in exactly the failing regime it exists for.
- **Fired symptoms retired:** #46 (overnight self-heal counts only
  outcome=='respawned' — a persistently failing spawn retries forever,
  DEAD flip never fires, attempts invisible in the morning brief), #69
  (auto-resume bumps the cap counter after the resubmit with no
  try/except — a failed write both crashes the monitor tick and leaves
  the counter stale-LOW, inverting the comment's safety claim).
- **Diagnosis cost:** sweep-caught; the failure regime is overnight —
  the highest-cost blindness window.
- **Upstream alternative:** attempts are counted durably BEFORE the act
  and include failure outcomes; a failed attempt can only over-count
  (escalate sooner = safe). One rule, applied wherever a cap gates
  retries.
- **Migration cost:** low.
- **Flip trigger:** immediate.
- **Confidence:** medium.
